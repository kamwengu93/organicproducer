Welcome to Organic Produce Kenya

Organic Produce Kenya is a FREE online resource centre where buyers and sellers meet to trade organic farm produce as well as exchange knowledge on sustainable organic farming.

Get Started

At a time when lifestyle diseases are on the rise due to poor nutrition, we intend to mobilize and promote sustainable organic farming accross the country.

According to the World Health Organization, Noncommunicable diseases (NCDs) kill 41 million people each year, equivalent to 74% of all deaths globally.

Each year, 17 million people die from a NCD before age 70; 86% of these premature deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries.

WHY ORGANIC?

Organic food can be more expensive than non-organic, but is it better for you or us and our planet? These are the reasons why we strongly propagate organic living

Low Chemicals.

It was discovered that conventional crops have pesticide residues four times more frequently. Additionally, organic crops had 48 percent lower levels of the hazardous element cadmium than conventional ones, despite the fact that cadmium is known to cause cancer in people.

Better Nutrition

According to a study in the British Journal of Nutrition â€“ organic foods have higher concentrations of antioxidants than conventionally grown foods. Antioxidants have previously been linked to a reduced risk of chronic diseases, including neurodegenerative diseases and certain cancers.

Environmental Protection

Organic farming promotes soil health and therefore reduces soil degradation. It also causes less pollution to rivers and waterways than conventional farming. According to the Soil Association, adopting nature-friendly farming and other key changes to the food system could keep global warming below 2Â°C.

Animal Welfare

The Soil Association standards for organic farmers ensure that animals are given plenty of space, fresh air, access to pasture and kept in conditions that suit their natural behaviour. As a result, farmers avoid giving them hormones and antibiotics. Treating livestock ethically is one of the fundamental principles of organic food production.

Objectives

Marketing

By assisting them in locating both domestic and foreign markets for their produce, we hope to increase the motivation and profits of organic farmers.

Training

To make sure the products and production methods meet international industry standards, we provide training to our producers and those who are interested in switching from non-Organic to Organic productions.

Networking

We design events that provide Kenyan organic farmers the chance to network with prospective clients both domestically and internationally. Producers get to know one another and one other's work naturally at these occasions. For Kenyan organic producers, these networking opportunities have opened up a lot of doors and will continue to do

Extension Services

We make sure our farmers and producers stay up to date on all the newest developments so they can continue to build on the progress they have already made.

Advocacy

We tackle all policy concerns of our farmers as they arise using our lobby groups that regularly interacts with law and policy makers. We ensure that all regulations take into consideration the welfare of our farmers.

Certification and Standards

If we want to compete, Kenyan organic produce must continually meet international standards. For this reason, we strive to raise standards and certify our producers appropriately.
